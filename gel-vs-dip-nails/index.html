Who should get gel?

Who should get dip?

  • ✦ You want longer-lasting color with less upkeep
  • ✦ You prefer to skip UV lamp exposure
  • ✦ You want a harder, more protective finish

Our Take

Most clients who want natural color with no fuss go dip. Clients who love nail art or want a softer, more flexible feel go gel. When in doubt, just ask us when you come in — we'll tell you honestly which one suits your nails better.

Questions

Gel vs. Dip FAQ

Is gel or dip better for weak nails?

Dip is generally the better choice for weak or brittle nails. The powder adds structure and thickness that helps protect the natural nail underneath. Gel is thinner and more flexible — great for healthy nails, but won't add the same reinforcement.

Which lasts longer, gel or dip nails?

Dip typically lasts 3–5 weeks before you see growth showing at the base. Gel runs 2–3 weeks before lifting or chipping starts. If you want to go longer between appointments, dip is the practical choice.

Does gel damage your nails more than dip?

Neither causes significant damage when done and removed correctly. The real risk with both is picking them off — that's what damages the nail underneath. Always soak off properly, and your nails will be fine with either option.

Can I switch from gel to dip at my next appointment?

Yes, no problem. Just let us know when you book or when you arrive. There's nothing complicated about switching — we'll remove what's on and start fresh with your new choice.

Do you offer both gel and dip at Nail'd It!?

Which is cheaper — gel or dip at Nail'd It!?

Gel manicure is $35. Dip/SNS full set is $40. Gel costs a little less upfront, but dip lasts longer — so the cost per week is actually very similar. Pick based on what works for your nails, not just the price.
