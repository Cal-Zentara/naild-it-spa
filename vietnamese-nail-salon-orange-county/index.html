The History

A Family Tradition

The Vietnamese nail industry has a history spanning decades. When actress Tippi Hedren helped Vietnamese refugees learn the trade in the 1970s, it sparked a craft that became a cultural cornerstone. Today, Vietnamese-owned nail salons represent over 50% of the nail industry in the United States — built on precision, care, and community. That reputation wasn't handed to them. It was earned, one client at a time, across generations.
