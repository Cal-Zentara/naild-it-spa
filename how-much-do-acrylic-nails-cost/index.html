Context

Why Acrylic Prices Vary

A full set can run anywhere from $30 to $100+ depending on where you go and what you ask for. The main factors that move the price:

  • ✦ Shape & length — longer lengths and complex shapes (coffin, stiletto) take more time and product
  • ✦ Salon quality — product brand, tech experience, and how much time is taken per set
  • ✦ Nail art — any design added is priced on top of the base service
  • ✦ Location — salons in high-rent areas charge more to cover overhead

Maintenance

Fill vs. Full Set — What's the Difference?

A lot of people come in not knowing whether they need a fill or a full set. Here's the simple version:

Service What It Is When You Need It
Fill Fills in the gap that forms as your natural nail grows out. Keeps your existing extensions intact. Every 2–3 weeks after your full set
Full Set Starts completely from scratch. All extensions replaced. Every 6–8 weeks, or when extensions are lifted/damaged

If you're coming in for the first time or your acrylics have lifted significantly, you'll need a full set. Ask us when you call — we'll tell you which one applies to your situation before you book.

Worth Knowing

Is Cheaper Always Better?

No. Low-cost acrylics often mean rushed application, cheaper product, or both. That usually shows up as lifting within the first week or uneven structure that breaks easily.

At Nail'd It! Spa, we use quality product and take time on every set. The goal is nails that look good and stay on — not a quick turnaround. Our $50 full set reflects that. Ask our clients on Yelp if you want a second opinion.

Acrylic Nail FAQ

How much does a full set of acrylic nails cost in Stanton?

A full set at Nail'd It! Spa is $50. That includes shaping, extension, cuticle cleanup, and base color. Nail art is available as an add-on from $5 per nail.

How often do I need an acrylic fill?

Most clients get a fill every 2–3 weeks. That's when the gap at the base of the nail from natural growth becomes visible. Waiting longer than 3 weeks can cause lifting and may require a full new set instead.

What's the difference between a fill and a full set?

A fill covers the grown-out gap and freshens your existing extensions without removing them. A full set starts from scratch — all extensions replaced. Most clients need a fill first, then a full new set every 6–8 weeks.

Does nail art cost extra for acrylic nails?

Yes — nail art is an add-on, starting at $5 per nail. Price goes up with design complexity. Simple accent nails stay low. Full custom sets are priced per session. We'll quote you before starting so you know the total upfront.

Are your acrylic prices all-inclusive?

The $50 full set includes shaping, extension, cuticle cleanup, and base color. Nail art and any special finishes are additional. We tell you the total before we start — no surprise charges at checkout.
