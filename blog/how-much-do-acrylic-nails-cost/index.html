The price of acrylic nails varies more than most people expect. You'll find full sets listed anywhere from $30 to $100 depending on the salon, the location, and what you're asking for. If you're trying to figure out what's fair, what to budget, and what you actually get for the price — this breaks it down.

At Nail'd It! Spa in Stanton, our full acrylic set starts at $50. Here's what that number means, and how to think about pricing across the board.

What's Included in the Price of a Full Set

A full set means starting from scratch — no existing extensions, just your natural nails. The price should cover everything needed to build a complete set. At Nail'd It, $50 includes:

  • Custom shaping to the length and shape you want
  • Length extension on all ten nails
  • Cuticle cleanup and nail prep
  • Base color of your choice

Nail art is not included in that base price — it's an add-on starting at $5 per nail depending on the design. We always tell you the total before we start. No surprise charges at checkout.

How Much Do Acrylic Nail Fills Cost?

Fills are how you maintain your set between full replacements. Instead of removing everything and starting over, your nail tech fills in the gap at the cuticle where your natural nail has grown out.

Across Orange County, fills typically run $25 to $40. The exact cost depends on the salon, how much growth there is, and whether any nails need repair. Most clients come in every 2 to 3 weeks for a fill — it's less expensive than a new set and keeps your nails looking fresh.

Nail Art and Add-Ons: What to Expect

Nail art is priced per nail at most salons, and the range is wide. A simple accent nail is far less than a full custom design across all ten. At Nail'd It, add-on nail art starts at $5 per nail. The final price depends on the design complexity — we'll quote you before starting.

  • Add-on Nail art from $5 per nail

  • Add-on Extra length beyond standard extension
  • Included Base color with every full set

What Makes Acrylic Nail Prices Go Up

A few things push the cost higher regardless of which salon you go to:

Length Longer nails require more product and more time to shape. Extra-long sets are priced higher at most salons.
Nail art complexity Hand-painted designs, 3D elements, or full-coverage custom art take significantly longer than a base color.
Repairs needed If nails have lifting, cracks, or damage that needs to be addressed before the fill, expect the price to reflect that extra work.
Special finishes Chrome powder, cat-eye gel, or specialty gel topcoats are add-ons that come with their own cost.

Acrylic Nail Prices in Orange County

Orange County nail salon pricing sits a bit higher than the national average, which runs around $45 for a full set according to industry data from the Professional Beauty Association. In OC, $50 to $70 for a quality set is standard. Anything below $35 is worth questioning — not because cheap is always bad, but because acrylic nail work takes time, and time has to be accounted for somewhere.

At Nail'd It, $50 reflects real product, real technique, and the time it takes to do it right. Dalena and the team — Twee, Nancy, Mindy — have been doing this for years. The consistency is part of why clients keep coming back.

Why the Cheapest Price Isn't Always the Best Deal

A $30 full set and a $60 full set are not the same service. Lower prices usually mean one of a few things: faster work (less shaping precision), thinner product (lifts faster), or lower-grade acrylic (more likely to yellow or crack). The consequence shows up two weeks later when you need an unexpected repair, or when the nails start lifting before your scheduled fill.

The real cost of nails isn't just what you pay at the desk — it's how long they look good and how your natural nails hold up underneath. Good technique protects the nail bed. Rushing through a $30 set can lead to damage that takes months to grow out.
